# Does garlic lower blood pressure?

Garlic has a folk reputation as a heart food that, unusually, holds up when you pool the randomized
trials. It is one of the better-studied foods for blood pressure specifically — and two newer
meta-analyses (2023 and 2024) put it back in the frame, this time as part of a broader effect on
metabolic-syndrome risk factors. Here is what the pooled evidence actually shows.

## The likely mechanism

The active story starts with damage. An intact garlic clove contains no allicin; crushing or chopping
it lets the enzyme alliinase meet its substrate and form **allicin**, the pungent sulfur compound.
Allicin and its breakdown products act as donors of two gas signalling molecules — **hydrogen sulfide
(H₂S)** and **nitric oxide (NO)**. Both tell the smooth muscle in the artery wall to relax, which
widens the vessel (vasodilation). A wider, more relaxed vessel means blood pushes less hard against
its walls — lower blood pressure. It is a different chemistry from beetroot's
[dietary-nitrate pathway](/blog/why-beetroot-lowers-blood-pressure), but the endpoint — more nitric
oxide, more relaxed arteries — is the same.

## What the pooled trials found

- The dedicated blood-pressure meta-analysis remains the clearest read: pooling **17 randomized
  trials**, garlic supplements lowered systolic pressure by about **3.75 mmHg** (95% CI −5.04 to
  −2.45) and diastolic pressure by about **3.39 mmHg** (95% CI −4.14 to −2.65). The effect was
  largest in people who were already hypertensive — roughly **4.4 mmHg** off systolic pressure in
  that subgroup ([Rohner et al., 2015](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25557383/)).
- A 2024 systematic review and meta-analysis of garlic in metabolic syndrome found significant
  reductions in **both systolic and diastolic** blood pressure (both p < 0.00001), alongside
  improvements in fasting glucose, cholesterol, triglycerides and waist circumference — leading the
  authors to describe garlic as anti-hypertensive, anti-hyperlipidaemic and anti-hyperglycaemic
  ([Varade et al., 2024](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37517570/)).
- A 2023 meta-analysis and meta-regression of **19 trials (999 participants)** likewise found garlic
  improved several metabolic-syndrome components, including a significant fall in diastolic pressure
  (standardised mean difference −1.33, 95% CI −2.14 to −0.53), plus lower triglycerides, LDL and
  waist circumference ([Fu et al., 2023](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37481521/)).

The two newer reviews measure garlic inside the wider metabolic-syndrome picture rather than isolating
blood pressure, but they point the same direction as the dedicated 2015 analysis: a real, repeatable,
modest reduction.

## The honest caveats

- **The effect is modest.** A few mmHg is meaningful at a population level and stacks with other
  habits, but it is not a substitute for the basics — sodium, weight, activity, sleep, and any
  medication a clinician has prescribed.
- **These trials used supplements, not seasoning.** The evidence comes largely from aged garlic
  extract and standardised garlic powder tablets at doses well above what you would sprinkle on
  dinner. A clove or two in cooking is healthy, but it is not the same intervention that was tested.
- **The newer meta-analyses report standardised effect sizes, not mmHg.** They confirm the
  *direction* and significance across many trials, but the 2015 analysis is the better source for how
  big the blood-pressure change actually is.
- **Heterogeneity is real.** Individual trials disagree, which is exactly why pooling them matters —
  and why this is best read as a gentle, supportive effect rather than a guaranteed one.

## The takeaway

Across old and new pooled evidence, garlic nudges blood pressure down by a few mmHg, most reliably in
people who are already hypertensive and when taken as a concentrated supplement. Treat it as a helpful
addition to the fundamentals, not a replacement — and talk to a clinician about managing blood
pressure. Read the full entry on [garlic](/items/garlic), or browse the other
[foods for high blood pressure](/goals/high-blood-pressure).
