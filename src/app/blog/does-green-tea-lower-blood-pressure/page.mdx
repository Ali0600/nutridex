# Does green tea lower blood pressure?

Green tea is best known on this site for its [cholesterol and focus effects](/items/green-tea), but
it turns up again and again in cardiovascular research. On blood pressure specifically, individual
trials have pointed in different directions — so the useful question is what happens when you pool
them. Several meta-analyses, including a GRADE-assessed one from 2025, have now done exactly that, and
the picture that emerges is consistent but deliberately unglamorous: a **small, real** downward nudge.

## The likely mechanism

Green tea is rich in **catechins**, chiefly **EGCG** (epigallocatechin gallate). The leading
explanation for a blood-pressure effect is that these polyphenols support **nitric-oxide
availability** in the lining of blood vessels and reduce oxidative stress there. Nitric oxide signals
the vessel wall to relax and widen (vasodilation), and a more relaxed vessel means blood pushes less
hard against it. This is the same endpoint that [beetroot reaches by a different
route](/blog/why-beetroot-lowers-blood-pressure) — dietary nitrate rather than polyphenols — and that
[pomegranate's punicalagins](/blog/does-pomegranate-lower-blood-pressure) are thought to reach too.
Green tea's catechins may also mildly improve blood lipids and body weight, which can move blood
pressure indirectly.

## What the pooled trials found

- The most rigorous to date is a 2025 **GRADE-assessed dose-response meta-analysis of 36 randomized
  controlled trials**. Pooled, green tea supplementation lowered systolic blood pressure by about
  **1.1 mmHg** (95% CI: −1.98 to −0.18) and diastolic by about **1.1 mmHg** (95% CI: −1.67 to −0.50).
  The effect was larger in people who started with elevated blood pressure. The authors are explicit
  that the trials were highly heterogeneous and that green tea is "better considered a complementary
  approach" ([Rezaei et al., 2025](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40497293/)).
- A 2023 meta-analysis restricted to **healthy individuals (9 RCTs, 680 people)** — chosen to strip
  out confounders — found a somewhat larger effect: systolic pressure down **2.99 mmHg** and diastolic
  down **0.95 mmHg** ([Yıldırım Ayaz et al., 2023](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36689359/)).
- Zooming out, a 2022 **umbrella review pooling 13 earlier meta-analyses** concluded that regular green
  tea has beneficial effects across several cardiometabolic markers — blood pressure among them, along
  with weight, blood glucose, and lipids — while stressing that the size of the benefit depends on how
  much you drink and your overall diet and activity ([Neyestani et al.,
  2022](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35750605/)).

## The honest caveats

- The effect is **small** — roughly 1 to 3 mmHg, at the lower end of food-based approaches. For context,
  that's less than [pomegranate juice](/blog/does-pomegranate-lower-blood-pressure) or
  [beetroot](/blog/why-beetroot-lowers-blood-pressure) typically show. It's a supportive habit alongside
  the basics (sodium, weight, activity, and any prescribed medication), not a replacement for them.
- **Heterogeneity is high.** The trials disagree with each other a lot (I² around 74–85% in the 2025
  analysis), and the dose-duration relationship wasn't clean — which is precisely why the GRADE authors
  hedged their conclusion. Pooling reveals a direction, not a precise dose.
- **Most trials used supplements or brewed extracts, not a single mug.** The doses that were studied
  often exceed what one casual cup delivers, and green tea also contains caffeine, which can transiently
  *raise* blood pressure in people who aren't habitual consumers.
- Individual results vary, and the benefit looks largest in those who already have elevated pressure.
  Talk to a clinician about managing blood pressure — this is general information, not medical advice.

## The takeaway

Pooled across dozens of randomized trials, regular green tea appears to lower blood pressure by a
modest few mmHg, most reliably in people whose pressure is already elevated. It's a pleasant,
low-calorie habit that stacks with the fundamentals rather than standing in for them. Read the full
entry on [green tea](/items/green-tea), or browse the other [foods for high blood
pressure](/goals/high-blood-pressure).
